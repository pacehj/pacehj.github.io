Overview

Summary

This is a design that shows my understanding of how to create an attention grabbing landing page.

Project Scope

Problem

Design a landing page that promotes a live Beyoncè performance at a bowling alley.

Solution

Created an enticing,simplistic, and elegant landing page design that utilized color psychology and microcopy.

Research

Who are the Users?

My research helped me to determine that the majority of Beyoncè fans are between the ages of 25-40 years old. This allowed me to start brainstorming the aesthetic of the landing page. Since this event would be for an older crowd, I wanted to convey romance and maturity.

What do They Want?

This event is a once in a lifetime opportunity (imagine being this close to your favorite musician). The users want to be able to easily scan important information on the landing page. They also want to know where to click in order to buy tickets immediately.

Design

Color Psychology

The primary color I selected for this design was pink. Pink was the best option for this concept because it symbolizes passion, nostalgia, and can create urgency. I also was inspired to use pink to create a sense of familiarity because Beyoncè's "Formation Tour" incorporated pink.

Font Style

I decided to use Staatliches as my header font. This was because it's a font with strong bold letters that are very easy to read. Similarly, I used Overpass as my body font because of readability. Since this font is san-serif users are able to read and comprehend the information faster than if I were to use a serif font.

Iteration 1

I started my design process by sketching and writing ideas. I began to think about how I wanted my header text to look. I decided to go with a neon theme because throughout this project I kept thinking about galaxy bowling. I also began to sketch my landing page layout. I wanted to have a scroll web page that contained 3 sections. For each one of these sections, my plan was to create vector drawings that would direct the user's attention to call to action buttons and important text.

After sketching, I decided to create a few low fidelity wireframes in order to get a better idea of how I wanted to layout each section.

I then began to play with font concepts and finalized the vector drawings.

This is the first iteration of the landing page design. The page has three sections. A home section that contains the venue name, date, and minimum age for entrance. The second section offers a call to action for users to buy tickets. The last section offers users the ability to subscribe to the bowling alley's weekly newsletter.

Feedback

After my first iteration the feedback I received was to condense information into just one section so that the landing page would have no scrolling. After thinking more about the user, I realized that they really don't care about any information that does not discuss Beyoncè.

Iteration 2

This is the second iteration of my design. I focused on adjusting the layout in order to include all information. I also played around with the sizing and shape of the call to action button.
